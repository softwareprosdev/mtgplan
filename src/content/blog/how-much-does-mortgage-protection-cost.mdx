## How Much Does Mortgage Protection Insurance Cost?

Mortgage protection insurance typically costs between $20 and $70 per month for most homeowners, though your actual premium depends on your age, health, loan amount, and term length. A healthy 40-year-old with a $250,000 mortgage balance can often find coverage in the $35–$50 per month range. Older homeowners or those with health conditions may pay more, while younger, healthier applicants often pay less.

The good news: mortgage protection is one of the most affordable forms of life insurance available, and many policies require no medical exam.

## What Factors Affect the Cost of Mortgage Protection Insurance?

### 1. Your Age

Age is the single biggest factor in determining your MPI premium. The older you are when you apply, the higher the statistical risk — and the higher your rate. Locking in coverage early, while you're younger, is one of the best ways to secure a lower premium for the life of your policy.

### 2. Your Health Status

Many mortgage protection policies offer simplified underwriting, meaning you answer a short health questionnaire rather than taking a physical exam. However, your answers still affect pricing. Serious conditions like heart disease, cancer history, or diabetes may result in higher rates or limited coverage options.

For homeowners who have been declined elsewhere due to health issues, mortgage protection insurance often remains available when other policies are not.

### 3. Your Mortgage Balance

The larger your outstanding loan, the more coverage your policy needs to provide — and the higher your premium. A $400,000 mortgage will cost more to insure than a $150,000 mortgage, all else being equal.

### 4. Your Loan Term Remaining

Policies are typically written to match your remaining mortgage term. If you have 30 years left, your policy runs 30 years. If you're 15 years into a 30-year mortgage, coverage for the remaining 15 years will be less expensive than coverage for a brand-new 30-year term.

### 5. Coverage Add-Ons (Riders)

Some policies include optional riders that affect price:
- **Disability rider:** Covers mortgage payments if you become unable to work (typically adds $5–$15/month)
- **Job loss rider:** Covers payments if you're involuntarily unemployed (varies by insurer)
- **Return of premium rider:** Returns premiums paid if you outlive the policy (significantly higher cost)

## How Does Mortgage Protection Compare to Term Life Insurance in Cost?

For young, healthy applicants, traditional term life insurance is often cheaper per dollar of coverage — but it requires a full medical exam and a longer underwriting process. Mortgage protection insurance trades some cost efficiency for accessibility and speed.

For applicants in their 50s or with health conditions, the difference narrows significantly. MPI's simplified underwriting means those who might pay sky-high term life premiums (or be declined entirely) can still access affordable home protection.

## 5 Ways to Lower Your Mortgage Protection Insurance Premium

### 1. Apply While You're Young and Healthy

Every year you wait increases your risk profile. If you just bought a home and you're in good health, now is the best time to lock in a low rate.

### 2. Choose a Decreasing Benefit Policy

A decreasing benefit policy (where coverage tracks your loan balance) is less expensive than a level-benefit policy. Since you're specifically protecting your mortgage, this structure makes sense for most homeowners.

### 3. Skip Riders You Don't Need

Each added rider increases your premium. Review what's included and opt out of riders that duplicate coverage you already have elsewhere.

### 4. Avoid the Return-of-Premium Rider (Unless It Fits Your Budget)

The return-of-premium rider sounds appealing, but it can double your monthly cost. For most homeowners, the base policy provides more value for less money.

### 5. Compare Multiple Quotes

Rates vary considerably between insurers. Getting multiple quotes takes only a few minutes and can save you hundreds of dollars per year over the life of your policy.

## Is Mortgage Protection Insurance Worth the Cost?

Consider the math: if a $45/month policy protects a $300,000 mortgage, you're paying $540/year for protection against a $300,000 liability. If you passed away or became disabled without coverage, your family could lose their home. For most homeowners, this is one of the highest-value insurance purchases available.

The question isn't really "can I afford mortgage protection insurance?" — it's "can my family afford to be without it?"
